# Impeccable Design Context

## Design Context

### Users
Potential employers, design directors, technical co-founders, and startup recruiters in the tech/design industry. They browse the portfolio quickly on desktop, often comparing multiple candidates, looking for evidence of taste, technical depth, and shipping ability. The visitor already knows what design is — they're evaluating whether Melvin's work meets their bar.

### Brand Personality
**Three words**: Precise. Uncompromising. Shipped.

Melvin is a founding-level product designer who codes — not a traditional portfolio case study presenter. The brand should feel like a design director's own tool: dark, minimal, architectural. Editorial without being precious. Zero decoration for its own sake.

### Aesthetic Direction
- **Theme**: Dark mode — this is a portfolio for someone who builds at night, deploys at midnight, works in terminal windows
- **Visual tone**: Editorial/architectural dark. Think Dezeen in dark mode crossed with a Bloomberg terminal. Not "cool hacker," not "creative agency" — precise and controlled
- **Typography**: Bebas Neue (display) + body font TBD (Inter currently — flagged as reflex font)
- **Color palette**: Near-black background (#0a0a0a), warm cream text (#e8ddd0), warm gold accent (#c4a882)
- **Anti-references**: Glassmorphism portfolios, gradient-heavy Dribbble aesthetics, card grids, centered everything

### Design Principles
1. **Everything earns its place** — no decoration without function; no copy without purpose
2. **Precision over polish** — tight, controlled layouts over soft/rounded everywhere
3. **Dark without glowing** — dark mode as a considered choice, not a default mood board
4. **Ship signals** — every design choice should signal someone who has actually shipped products
5. **Text is the design** — typography does the heavy lifting; icons and decoration are minimal and purposeful
